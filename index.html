Features & Functionalities

Verifiable Immunity Passport (VIP)

Securely deliver a patient’s test results and relevant medical history to their VIP on Immunodex. This generates a trusted, tamper-proof credential that can be used to validate their fitness to work or travel.

Secure & Fraud-Proof

Decentralized identifiers (DID) of credential holders and issuers with peer-reviewed cryptographic techniques ensures people really are who they claim to be.

Accurate Epidemiological Monitoring

Ensure that data from screening programmes is unalterable and completely auditable. Data profiles can be tailored to specific objectives or requirements.

EU GDPR Compliant

User-centric design means patients hold, own and control their own data, exchanging it with other parties only upon consent. With zero-knowledge proof, patients don’t have to disclose more than what’s needed.

Rapid Implementation

Implement our technology at pandemic speed. Any camera-enabled device can issue and verify credentials. Screening criteria can be customized based on your company's requirements.

Coordinate Patient Flow

Streamline the scheduling, reminding and follow-up of testing appointments. Availabilities can be adapted to changes in patient volume and acuity.

Decentralized Network

We use a globally distributed self-sovereign identity (SSI) network to ensure privacy-preserving credentials.

User-Friendly Design

For patients, the VIP runs as a secure mobile app with an easy-to-use interface. Information is exchanged by simply scanning QR codes.

Frequently Asked Questions

Am I giving up my privacy and freedom in exchange for "safety?"

Actually, the opposite. You are directly contributing to everyone's safety and indirectly contributing to your own by using verifiable credentials: you are also increasing your privacy beyond what privacy regulations in most places require. This exceeds what companies are required by law to do today! It is a consent-based system. You have the freedom to share your information to a valid inspector or refuse, on a case-by-case basis.


Immunodex indirectly contributes to your safety by letting policy makers in a company or government know about the health of their people in a timely and reliable manner. In order to do this, the "paper trail" of the health records must be traceable. This trail rightfully belongs to you. But right now, it does not. It sits on your doctor's computers, or the hospital's servers, or the government has it. You have no access to your own health data, but we let you hold your own data. Finally, the inspection is not only consent-based, but requires a minimum exchange of information, which means you might not need to reveal ALL data, but simply fulfil the requirements of the inspector (this is "zero-knowledge proof").

We don't know about the immunology, whether having antibodies actually confers long term immunity to Covid-19. Also, the antibody test market is full of scams!

Yes, you are right. And it doesn't matter. Immunodex is a bias-free, completely neutral historian that records personal health data for that person. Immunodex is starting off with use cases concerning Covid-19 because that is important and urgent. New research and findings do not invalidate records on Immunodex. In fact, reliable records contribute to ongoing research.

There are many moral and ethical implications! Will this create classes of people? This may set a dangerous precedent.

This is a question for behavioural scientists or some experts in psychology or economics. We are not the right people to ask and it doesn't matter what we think. This is like asking the founders of LinkedIn if they think people would lie on their LinkedIn profiles to get a job. Except that LinkedIn does not even have the means to verify information on its system. Immunodex does.


We agree that there might be moral implications. Our goal however is to give people control of their own health data (this is called "self-sovereignty"). It is controversial because some of the incumbents have not understood how to survive in a world where they have to ask for consent from people to use their data, instead of doing whatever they want after taking it. They would rather lobby for the government to relax privacy regulations, and the government does not realize systems like Immunodex exist — systems that exceed the privacy requirements of existing regulations.


The precedent that is being set here is (1) data must be accountable, and (2) data access must be consent-based. So this precedent is only dangerous for those who can't adapt to a world where they must ask for permission to use people's data.

What happens if I lose/break my phone? How do I access the "backup?"

The patient or authorized person (“Holder”) primarily holds the information on their computer or mobile device(s), and the information should be backed up (location is the Holder's choice). A back-up is an encrypted file that is an exact copy of the one on the main device(s). The Holder accesses their information (working copy and backups) via a key. If the key is lost, then the patient undergoes a Key Recovery Process, which is similar to the onboarding process.

What happens if I got a result from a test kit that was later recalled/nullified by the government?

Your record remains with you forever. Immunodex is independent of policies from government or authorities. Inspectors requesting proof-of-health will see not only the result but also that the test kit used is or is not on the [currently] approved list of test kits.
