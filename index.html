Why current solutions don't work

Invasion of privacy

Contact-tracing surveillance apps compromise individual privacy and confidentiality. There is no transparency on how collected data is being manipulated.

Fraud & misguided incentives

Negative test results granting individuals work and social freedoms are being forged.

Lack of standardization

With public health uncertainty and jurisdictional heterogeneity, how can we trust a health record from another country?

Trust is the only long-term solution

Immunodex helps you trust your users' health and vice versa
so you can resume business safely.

Verifiable Health Passport (VHP)

The VHP is set of trusted, tamperproof credentials about a user's health that organizations can verify to grant them access to physical premises.

Privacy-preserving

Our user-centric design adheres to principles of the EU GDPR and the Pan-Canadian Trust Framework. Users hold, own and control their data, exchanging it with others only upon consent.

Secure & fraud-proof

Decentralized identifiers and peer-reviewed cryptographic techniques ensure that 3rd parties' claims are verifiable.

No more paperwork

Save resources with our completely digital solution. Data and statistical reports are completely auditable and readily formatted for Public Health.

Rapid implementation

Any camera-enabled device can issue and verify VHP credentials, and our technology can be readily customized.

Interoperable

We are the first Verifiable Credentials solution to use the HL7® FHIR® standards for health data interoperability.

Decentralized network

A global self-sovereign identity network with a distributed ledger registry safeguards health data beyond current standards.

User-friendly design

For your organization, the VHP runs as a secure mobile or tablet app with an easy-to-use interface. Information is exchanged via QR codes.

The user's VHP is ready for inspection

Use Cases

Back-to-Work
Employers who rely on in-person operations or physical interactions can allow staff recognized to be free from Covid-19 and in good health to return to the workplace. Staff and customers can be reassured that they are in premises posing a low risk of Covid-19 transmission.

Holder

Verifier

An employee
An employer (e.g. restaurant, shopping mall, grocery store, warehouse, etc.)
Back-to-School
Schools and daycares can allow students to return. Parents can rest assured knowing that their child is interacting with other certifiably healthy children and school staff. Coverage can be extended to include critical immunizations such as those for measles, pertussis, etc.

Holder

Verifier

An student, teacher or school employee
A school, nursery, daycare, university, etc
Back-to-Sky
Airlines, trains and transportation industries can resume carrying passengers, especially in proposed “travel bubbles” aimed at reviving the tourism industry. People can be allowed to cross borders upon presentation of a recognized proof-of-health that is tamperproof and verifiable. Likewise, hotels and hospitality sectors can resume serving guests.

Holder

Verifier

A tourist, cross-border commuter, traveller
Airport/train security control/check-in desk, border customs
Back-to-Clinic
Physicians and healthcare workers can return to hospitals and clinics, especially for non-emergency yet important services such as ophthalmology, dermatology, dentistry and elective surgeries. Long-term care homes housing a highly vulnerable demographic could better mobilize and prioritize staff based on their health status.

Holder

Verifier

A doctor, healthcare worker, clinician, patient or visitor of patient
A hospital, clinic, long-term care home
